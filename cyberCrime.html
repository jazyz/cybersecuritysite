Cyber Crime

The Issue:

Cybercriminals are all over the internet, waiting for some unsuspecting internet user to fall into their trap. Once you fall in, these criminals will steal your data and invade your privacy. Here are some ways they do that:

1. Phishing

Cybercriminals use techniques like phishing in order to gain access to your data. They pretend to be a trusted entity, and then they try to get you to click on emails, text messages, or links. Once they trick you, they can then steal your data, such as your bank accounts, email addresses, and more. They might even try to impersonate you! This is called identity theft, which has becoming increasingly common as new technologies develop.

2. Malware

Malware is another dangerous way for your private information to be stolen. If you are not careful, you could end up with malware or viruses on your computer, which are malicious softwares that can range from annoying pop-ups to covert bitcoin mining to scanning for personal information. These can make your life a living nightmare. You definitely don’t want this to happen to you.


The Solutions:

There are many measures that you can take to safeguard yourself against cyber crimes. Here are some ways to do so.

1. Be Vigilant

Always be skeptical. Don’t click on suspicious things and don’t visit sketchy websites. Be wary of common scams and things that sound too good to be true. Also, you can check if your email address has been breached by using a site called Have I Been Pwned? If you have been affected, use the strategy below.

2. Strong Passwords / Password Manager

One thing that you should be doing is using strong passwords for all your accounts, which will make it difficult for hackers to gain access. This usually involves the use of special characters and numbers, and it is also a good idea to use a different password for each account, in case one of them gets compromised. To help with this process, there are password managers out there which can generate passwords, monitor accounts for security breaches, suggest changing weak passwords, and sync your passwords between your computer and phone.

3. Anti-Virus

To protect yourself from viruses, you can use anti-virus softwares such as McAfee or Norton, which will scan your computer for viruses and deal with them for you. If you are on Mac, the built-in defense is usually good enough for most users.
