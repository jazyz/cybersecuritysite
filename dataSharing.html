Data Sharing

The Issue:

We share tons of data with all sorts of companies all the time, whether we know it or not. Unfortunately, our data might be misused or even stolen. Here are some ways that can happen.

1. Apps

Many of the apps you use on a regular basis may request info such as location details, phone numbers, and email addresses, but some may ask for more. This could include your phone camera, microphone, or contacts. If you were to allow access to all of the information on your device, it may result in your data being shared without your knowledge.

2. Social Media

Any information posted on social media may also be at risk if you are not careful. For instance, one example of a privacy scandal was the Cambridge Analytica story, in which data was used to manipulate voters, conduct cyberbullying, and dox other users. Many huge platforms have suffered data breaches, such as Facebook, where a massive data breach leaked the personal data of 533 million users, which included full names, phone numbers, locations, birth dates, bios, and email addresses.


The Solutions:

To prevent data from being misused or stolen, it is important to make sure that you only share personal information when absolutely necessary. Here are some ways to do so.

1. Limit Access

It is important to limit the access that certain apps have on info such as location, photos, or contacts. You should always consider if the app provider that is requesting this access is trustworthy, and make sure to read the terms and conditions so that you know what will be done with it. If you are worried, you can always deny access later in the app settings.

2. Share As Little As Possible

On social media, it might be tempting to post a lot of information about yourself because all your friends are doing so too. However, posting things such as your home address, phone number, and email address is unnecessary, and can lead to your data being leaked.
