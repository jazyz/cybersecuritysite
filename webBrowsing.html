Web Browsing

The Issue:

Every single thing you search can be seen by big tech companies that provide you their service. This seems a little invasive to your privacy, doesn't it? Here are some of the ways they track you.

1. Search Engines

Today, people across the world use search engines on a regular basis. Modern browsers track tons of stuff such as search history, IP addresses, and click through history. This information is sold and used for profiling, or making a customer persona. It is a serious privacy issue if someone’s profile gets associated with their personally identifiable information, because data breaches could happen. Also, sometimes, you just don’t want other people to see something you search, like "why do I have toe fungus?"

2. Cookies

Cookies are simply text files that monitor information about your computer and browsing behaviour. They can see everything you search and send that information without your knowledge to websites. We see the danger that comes with cookies, since they may submit sensitive data without explicit permission from the user. Right now, there are not sufficient laws in place to prevent against the threat of tracking cookies. How many times have you seen a website that claims cookies are essential to their function? This is an easy way for businesses to circumvent those legal provisions.


The Solutions:

So how can we protect our privacy when browsing the web? There are a variety of ways to do so.

1. VPN

In order to minimize the risk of having your information collected during web browsing, the best measure you can take is using a VPN. This creates a private network from a public internet connection. This will give you online privacy and anonymity and mask your IP address, so that it is impossible to trace your online actions. ExpressVPN is one great option.

2. Use Different Search Engine

You can also use a different search engine altogether such as DuckDuckGo. The advantage of DuckDuckGo is that it doesn't collect your data and track your searches. It doesn't associate your IP address with what you search, unlike search engines such as Google.

3. Cookie Blocking

There are ways to deal with annoying cookie trackers. Use cookie blocking extensions such as Ninja Cookie, which blocks all non-essential cookies.
